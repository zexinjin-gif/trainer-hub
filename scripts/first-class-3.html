First Class 3

The Experienced Member

Hook 0:00 to 0:15

So they said "yes, I have done this kind of training before."

Now what?

If you treat them like a beginner, they will leave. And they will not come back.

Because someone who has trained before can smell a coach who is not at their level. From the first sentence.

What They Actually Want 0:15 to 0:50

This person is not here to be welcomed in. This person is here to be challenged.

So before you do anything else, ask the right questions.

  • "What brings you here?"
  • "What have you been working on? Marathon? Hyrox?"
  • "Any injuries?"

Now you know if they are training for a Hyrox, recovering from an injury, or just want a place to push hard while they travel for work. Different person, different play.

Show Your Competence Early 0:50 to 1:40

This is where most trainers lose them.

In the first 5 minutes of the workout, you have to drop one coaching tip. One correction. One technical note. One observation about their movement that proves you actually see what they are doing.

Be specific.

"Let's change this exercise into a single-leg stand."
"Nah man, you can bench at least 25kg on those."

Experienced members are testing you. They want to know if you can actually see them. The moment you give them that one tip, you stop being just another trainer in the room.

You become a coach. Their coach.

Find The Gap 1:40 to 2:15

Now the membership question.

What can your program do that their previous training couldn't?

Maybe it's the consistency. 6am every single day, same time, no excuses. Maybe it's the community. Maybe it's the variety, because they got bored doing the same program for two years.

Whatever it is, find it. Then point to it. Don't pitch.

"You said you got bored. We change the focus every block. You will not see the same week twice."

That is what gets them to sign up.

Closing 2:15 to 2:30

So beginner or experienced, the play is completely different. But the goal is the same.

Make them feel seen. Make them feel coached. They come back.

That is how you build a gym. One new member at a time.
