Coaching Toolkit 3

How To Push The Client

Hook 0:00 to 0:15

The number one mistake every coach makes?

They push everyone the same way.

Same voice. Same energy. Same words.

It doesn't work.

Tool One: Know Who's In Front Of You 0:15 to 1:00

First rule. Look at your client.

A 40 year old mum. A 50 year old lady. A 60 year old man.

They don't need you screaming.

They need you to regress the exercise. Slow it down. Stand next to them. Have a chat while they move.

That's how you push them.

Now flip it.

A fit young guy. A fit young girl.

Get in there with them. Do the reps beside them. Count out loud. Use your coaching voice.

Same coach. Two completely different gears.

Push the person, not the program.

Tool Two: Set A Target 1:00 to 1:40

Second rule. Set targets.

"Faster. Stronger. Come on. Keep going."

That's noise. That tells the client you don't actually know what you're doing.

Give them a number.

"30 seconds. 15 calories on the bike. Three sets."

Now they have a finish line they can see.

And here's the part most coaches miss.

Watch their face.

If they're tired, drop the target.

Second set they did 12? Third set, ask for 10.

"Let's go for at least 10 for the last set."

They hit 11? Clap for them.

That's how you keep someone in the fight.

How To Use Both 1:40 to 1:55

Read the person. Set the number.

That's the whole thing.

Push hard when they can take it. Pull back the target when they can't.

Coaching is not about volume. It's about awareness.

Close 1:55 to 2:05

That's how you push the client without breaking them.
