Engagement 1

Who They Are

Hook 0:00 to 0:15

Engagement is priority number one.

Not the workout. Not the music. Not the screens. Engagement.

And I want to break engagement into two parts.

What Engagement Part 1 Is 0:15 to 0:40

Part one is simple. It's who they are.

Before you can coach someone properly, you need to actually know who you're coaching.

That means introducing yourself. Speaking to them. Getting to know them a little bit every single class.

This is the part that most new trainers skip. And this is the part that makes or breaks whether a member sticks around.

Introduce Yourself 0:40 to 1:05

First thing. If you don't know a member, introduce yourself.

Walk up to the members. Say your name. Shake their hand if it feels right. Ask for their name back.

"Hey, my name is Jino. Nice to meet you!"

It sounds basic, but this is the start of everything. A member who knows your name feels like they belong. A member who doesn't feel like they belong, they don't come back.

Learn Their Name and Use It 1:05 to 1:30

Once you know their name, use it. On the floor. During the warm up. At the end of class.

Names are everything. When we call out a correction and we use their name, it lands differently. When we celebrate a rep and we use their name, it feels personal.

If you forget, ask again. Nobody minds. What they do mind is being ignored.

Get To Know Them a Little 1:30 to 2:00

Next level. Every class, try to learn one new thing about a member.

What do they do for work. Do they have kids. Why are they here. What are they training for.

You don't need a deep conversation. Thirty seconds before or during the class is enough. Small pieces, every time you see them, build up into a real relationship.

The Every Paid Member Rule 2:00 to 2:30

And this is where I need to be really clear.

Engagement doesn't mean talking to the people you already like. Not the person you're comfortable with. Not someone your age. Not someone from your background.

It means every paid member. Every. Single. One.

If they walk through our doors and pay for our service, they deserve the same welcome, the same energy, the same name on your lips.

Challenge 2:30 to 2:50

So here's your challenge for the next class.

Pick one member you've never really spoken to. Walk up. Introduce yourself. Ask their name. Learn one thing about them.

That's it. One member, one conversation. Do that every class, and in a month you'll know the whole gym.

At the end of this month, I will randomly give you a name, and you need to tell me something about that member. Don't be the one who can't answer.

That's part one. Part two is where we talk about engagement during the actual coaching.
