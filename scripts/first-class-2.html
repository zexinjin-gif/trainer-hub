First Class 2

The Beginner Newbie

Hook 0:00 to 0:15

So they said "no, I have never done this before."

Now what?

Most trainers immediately rush to show the equipment. Start the workout. Big mistake.

Because if you actually want this person to sign up, the workout is NOT the most important thing. Not even close.

What Actually Matters 0:15 to 0:40

Before you teach them a single movement, there are two things you have to find out.

And the first one is more important than anything you will coach them all day.

So think about this for a second. What information, if you get it, gives you the best chance of turning a beginner into a paying member?

Think hard. What would you ask?

Question 1: Location, Location, Location 0:40 to 1:20

The answer is location.

The number one reason a beginner commits to a gym is not the class. It is not the coach. It is not the program.

It is how close they live.

A beginner who loves your gym but has to drive 30 minutes each way will cancel in a couple of months. Every time.

So the second question, right after the golden question from Part 1:

"Do you live around here? Are you local?"

One sentence. You will know almost straight away if this person is going to stick around.

Question 2: Make It About Them 1:20 to 2:05

Once you know they live close, there is one rule for the rest of that first class. Make everything about them. Not you. Not the gym. Not the program. Them.

Ask things like:

  • "What brings you in?"
  • "What made you want to start?"
  • "Tell me about your training history."
  • "Any exercises you are not comfortable with?"
  • "Is there anything you are worried about?"

Their answers are not really the point. The fact that you asked is the point. That is what they will think about on the drive home. That is what brings them back next week.

Then Do The Rest 2:05 to 2:25

Once you have those two things, where they live, and who they are, now you can do the regular stuff. The tour. The equipment. The sets, the reps, the timing, the program.

But keep connecting it back to them. "This exercise is exactly what you told me you wanted." "We will watch your form on that movement you said felt weird."

Everything comes back to them.
