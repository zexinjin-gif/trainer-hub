Engagement 4

Delivering The Golden Point

Hook 0:00 to 0:15

Last video I gave you the Golden Point.

Today: how to actually use it.

The Three Windows 0:15 to 1:00

One. The door. The thirty seconds before they put their water bottle down.

"Hey Sarah, how did your kid's soccer match go?"

They're inside the room, already feel seen, the class hasn't even started.

Two. The rest. Mid-set, catching breath. You walk past, low voice, one line.

"Did you go heavier on the deadlift today?"

Now they're locked into the next set.

Three. The way out.

"Same time next week?" or "Tell me how the date goes."

That's the line they remember on the way home.

Pre-workout. Mid-rest. Post-workout. That's it.

How To Deliver Without Breaking Flow

Three rules.

One reference, not five. Otherwise it sounds like an interrogation.

Use their actual words. If they said "kids", you say "kids". Don't upgrade to "children". The specifics are the proof you were listening.

Move on. Four seconds, max. The Golden Point is a touchpoint, not a conversation. The class doesn't slow down. The energy stays up.

The Graceful Recovery 1:35 to 1:55

What if you forget?

You will. Everyone does.

Don't fake it. Honest is better.

"Remind me, what was the thing you had on last week?"

Members respect being asked. They hate being forgotten.

The recovery itself becomes a Golden Point. Because it shows you actually want to know.

Close 1:55 to 2:05

Find it. Use it at the door, in the rest, on the way out. Recover when you fumble.

That's the Golden Point in motion.
