Engagement 2

Coaching The Room

Hook 0:00 to 0:10

Part two of engagement. This is where it gets real.

Part one was knowing who they are. Part two is coaching them once the timer starts.

Engagement Is Coaching 0:10 to 0:35

A lot of trainers think coaching is just counting reps and counting down the clock. It's not.

Everyone in that gym wants to be watched. Every single one of them.

If a member finishes a class and you didn't say their name, didn't fix their form, didn't give them a cue, didn't check in, that's a miss. Simple as that.

Use Their Name 0:35 to 0:55

First rule on the floor. Use their name.

"Nice work, Sarah." "Chest up, James." "Two more, Mel, you've got it."

When we use names, the member knows we're watching them. Not the room. Them. One word changes how hard they push and how much they trust us.

Correct Without Being a Jerk 0:55 to 1:20

Second rule. Correct form without being rude.

Nobody wants to feel told off in front of the class. So keep it short, keep it light.

Quick pattern: Name. Fix. Encourage.

"Tom, hips a bit lower. Yes, that's it, perfect."

Three seconds, done. They feel coached, not corrected. That's the difference.

Read The Room 1:20 to 1:45

Third rule. Read the room.

Some members need a push. Some need you to back off. Some walked in after a bad day and just need a smile.

Watch the face, watch the body language. Someone about to quit? Stand next to them and do it with them.

You can't do any of this holding the iPad or scrolling your phone.

The Quiet Ones 1:45 to 2:15

And this one is the big one. The quiet ones.

Every class has them. They slip in, do the work, slip out. They don't ask for attention, so most trainers never give them any.

Those are the members who cancel first.

So we go to them first. Name. Check in. A cue mid-round. A "great work" at the end. They might not say much back. But they'll feel it. And they'll come back.

Challenge 2:15 to 2:30

Here's your challenge.

Before you walk on the floor, pick three members, especially ones you barely coached before. By the end of that class, all three have heard their name, got a coaching point, and got a well done.

End of the month, I'll ask three random members if you coached them. Don't be the trainer they mention they never spoke with.

Do that every class. That's how we coach. That's the standard.
