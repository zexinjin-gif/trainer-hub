Coaching Toolkit 2

Correcting Forms

Hook 0:00 to 0:15

The workout is the easy part.

The hard part is fixing someone's form, in two seconds, without breaking their flow.

Tool One: Plain Language 0:15 to 1:00

First rule. Throw away your textbook.

Use simple language.

No concentric. No eccentric. No "elevate the scapula."

If they don't understand it, they won't do it.

You've got one breath between reps. Make it land.

Short. Sharp. One thing.

"Don't move your elbow."
"Slow on the way down."
"Lock your shoulders."
"Head up."

That's it. One word.

Confusing language is a waste of time.

The clearer you are, the faster they fix it.

Tool Two: Analogies 1:00 to 1:40

Second rule. Use analogies.

Members don't pay you to teach them muscle names. They pay you to help them feel it.

An analogy bridges their brain to their body.

Bench press? "Imagine you're pushing someone away from you."

Or get a laugh: "Push your boyfriend away when he's being annoying."

Now they get it. They feel the line of force without you ever saying "horizontal adduction."

A pull? "Like you're elbowing the person behind you."

A squat? "Sit into a chair that isn't there."

The picture lands faster than any textbook ever will.

How To Use Both 1:40 to 1:55

Two tools. Use them together.

A short cue. A quick image. Done.

Form correction isn't about being right. It's about being understood.

If they got it, you coached it.

Close 1:55 to 2:05

That's how you fix form without breaking the flow of the room.
