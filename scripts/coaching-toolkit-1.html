Coaching Toolkit 1

Get Your Mindset Right

Hook 0:00 to 0:15

Most coaches react.

The great ones predict.

On the floor, the difference is night and day.

Today: how to get your mindset right before you even step into the room.

Rule One: Proactive, Not Reactive 0:15 to 0:45

First rule. Be proactive, not reactive.

Reactive coaches wait for mistakes to happen. They scan the room and pick up what they see.

That's fine. But it's slow.

Proactive coaches go hunting.

You're actively searching for the bad rep before it goes bad. Scanning for the lazy moment before it shows up.

Hunting beats waiting. Every single time.

Rule Two: Predict The Mistakes 0:45 to 1:20

Second rule. Predict the mistakes before the class starts.

Look at the program. Read every exercise.

For each one, ask yourself: what's the most common thing people will do wrong here?

  • Heavy deadlift? They'll round their back.
  • Walking lunge? Knee will cave inward.
  • Overhead press? Ribs will flare.

Now you're not coaching cold. You're coaching with a checklist.

Rule Three: Prepare The Solutions 1:20 to 1:50

Third rule. For every predicted mistake, prepare the fix.

Don't just know what's wrong. Know exactly what you'll say.

Round back? "Chest up. Brace your stomach."
Knee caving? "Drive the knee out."
Ribs flaring? "Squeeze your stomach."

Now when it happens on the floor, you don't think. You just speak.

That's autopilot. That's a pro.

Rule Four: Get The Feedback 1:50 to 2:20

Fourth rule. After the class, get the feedback.

Some things will surprise you. A member who can't do a basic bicep curl. An advanced one you don't know how to push.

Stuff you'd never have predicted.

Write it down. After every session.

Not in your head. On paper. On your phone. Wherever you'll actually look back at it.

Then review it once a week.

You'll start spotting patterns. Same mistake, same fix, same kind of member.

That's how you compound. Don't skip it.

Close 2:20 to 2:30

Predict. Prepare. Note. Review.

That's the foundation every coaching tool sits on.
