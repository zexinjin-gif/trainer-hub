Sell On The Floor

Before they walk out, not after

The Golden Rule

Sell before they walk out the door. Not after.

While they're still in the studio, the energy of the room is hitting them. The music. The smiles. The high fives. They feel like they belong.

That's the buying moment.

Once they walk out the door, they're back in their day. Distracted. Mentally moved on. Whatever they were going to commit to in the studio is now "I'll think about it." And "I'll think about it" almost always means no.

Build The Connection First

Before any pricing talk, give them 60 seconds of real human attention.

  • Smile. Eye contact. Use your name and ask theirs.
  • Ask the golden question: "Have you done this kind of training before?"
  • Ask where they live. Proximity is the number one predictor of staying.
  • Ask what brings them in. Their goal becomes your hook.

If they feel seen, they want to come back. If they feel like a sales target, they don't.

Always Pitch Membership First

Lead with the weekly membership. $65/week, unlimited, ongoing.

Mention the sign-up bonus straight away: $69/week with an extra free week. That extra week is the hook. People love a bonus.

Frame it around their goal:

"You said you want to drop 5kg before summer. The members who get results train 3 to 4 times a week. At $65 unlimited, that's $5 a class. Cheaper than your morning coffee."

Drop The Ladder Slowly

If they hesitate, don't immediately drop to the cheapest option. Each step down loses urgency.

The order:

  1. Weekly Membership ($65/wk, $69 + free week)
  2. One Month Pass ($259) "test it for a month, no recurring"
  3. One Week Pass ($75) "quick taste"
  4. Class Packs ($275 for 10, $550 for 20) "for flexible schedules"
  5. ClassPass last resort, only if they're walking out

Handle "I Want To Think About It"

This is not a no. It's an unanswered question. Ask:

"Totally fair. What's holding you back?"

Then listen. Whatever they say (price, schedule, confidence), address it directly. Don't pitch over their concern.

If they still need time, lock in something today. Even a one-week pass keeps them engaged.

Use The Floor Energy

The minute the class wraps and people are high-fiving, the room is at peak energy. Use it.

Walk up to the new person. "How was that? Want to lock in another one this week?" If they're smiling, they'll say yes.

That's the close. Not a hard sell. A natural extension of the energy they're already feeling.

Last Note

The best salespeople in this gym are the ones who actually care about the person in front of them. Members can smell the difference between someone trying to hit a target and someone trying to help them change their life.

Coach the person, sell the membership. In that order.
